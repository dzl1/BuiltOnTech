What is Sine?

Sine is a trigonometric function. In a right triangle, sine of an angle is the ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle to the hypotenuse.

How to Use Sine

  • sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse
  • Example: If the opposite side is 3 and the hypotenuse is 5, sin(θ) = 3/5 = 0.6
